Eating Energy

Project Description:

This project seeks to visualise Australia’s energy consumption into a consumable material. Australia’s high non-renewable energy consumption is damaging to our picturesque environment and contributes to the ongoing climate crisis.

While Australia’s reliance on non-renewable energy consumption is hugely impactful on our environment, the effects aren’t necessarily obvious inside Australia largely urban population. Revelling the need for consumption data to be reimagined in order to infiltrate the Australian urban conversation.

We decided to encapsulate consumption data into a consumable food that sits at the centre of urban socialising, hummus. Hummus is common dip used as a light snack during social gatherings. Thus the insertion of consumption data with hummus means the otherwise distant issues is brought into the Australian urban environment and at the centre of conversation.

Below you will find a recipe generator that calculates ingredients to be added to base hummus recipe. The data is divided by state plus Australia as a whole (ACT in included in the national data not as an individual territory) from 2008 until 2018.

  • Non-Renewables
    • Coal = Coffee Powder
    • Natural Gas = Hundreds and Thousands
    • Oil = Maple Syrup
    • Other = Vanilla Essence
  • Renewables
    • Biomass = Coriander
    • Wind = Lemon Juice
    • Hydro = Cumin Powder
    • Solar PV = Garlic Powder
    • Geothermal = Paprika Powder

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 60g canned chickpeas, drained, rinse
  • 10ml of water
  • Turkish bread, toasted, to serve

Hummus Base Recipe

Step 1) Place the chickpeas, a pinch of salt, in a food processor and process until combined. Add 10mls of water and process again.

Step 2) Add data determined ingredients at their specific amounts, as seen as below. Stir well and serve.
